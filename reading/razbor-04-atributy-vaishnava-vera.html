По заявке участника Лаборатории — два тёплых, практических вопроса: что на самом деле означают внешние атрибуты вайшнава (шикха, тилака, туласи-мала) по Шриле Прабхупаде, и как усилить веру в Кришну. Ниже — только дословные цитаты из первоисточников (Прабхупада + ачарьи линии), каждая сверена на дословность; ссылки кликабельны.

Часть 1. Внешние атрибуты — их истинное значение

Коротко: по Прабхупаде это не «украшение» и не формальность, а три вещи сразу — знак принадлежности Кришне, защита, и предание. Но у всего этого есть красная линия (в конце части) — и она и есть «истинное значение».

Шикха — знак вайшнава и предания гуру

Шикха (прядь на затылке) — открытый знак того, что человек — вайшнава, слуга Кришны, и что он предал себя духовному учителю. Ею же освящают тело (Нараяна-кавача):

«if somebody dresses like nice American gentleman without any robes, I have no objection; but every one of my disciples must have the flag & marks of tilak on forehead. This is essential.»

671013 - Letter to Damodara written from Calcutta·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«so if we do not keep long hair & dress ourselves nicely with tilaka, flag & beads on the neck, apart from our devotional service, then certainly we shall be distinct from the Hippies.»

671017 - Letter to Pradyumna written from Calcutta·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«To date, all the devotees of Śrī Caitanya Mahāprabhu, following in His footsteps, accept the sannyāsa order and keep the sacred thread and tuft of unshaved hair. The ekadaṇḍi-sannyāsīs of the Māyāvādī school give up the sacred thread and do not keep any tuft of hair. Therefore they are unable to understand the purport of tridaṇḍa-sannyāsa, and as such they are not inclined to dedicate their lives to the service of Mukunda.»

Caitanya-caritamrta Madhya 3.6, комментарий Прабхупады·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«still the tridaṇḍī sannyāsīs are in existence, keeping the sacred thread and śikhā intact, distinct from the Māyāvādī ekadaṇḍī sannyāsīs, who give up the sacred thread and śikhā. They have no inclination to render service unto the Supreme Personality of Godhead.»

«One should place the syllable “oṁ” on his heart, the syllable “vi” on the top of his head, the syllable “ṣa” between his eyebrows, the syllable “ṇa” on his tuft of hair [śikhā], and the syllable “ve” between his eyes.»

Srimad-Bhagavatam 6.8.8-10 (Нараяна-кавача), перевод Прабхупады·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«The seven great sages [Marīci, Vasiṣṭha, Atri and so on] reside on planets beneath Dhruvaloka. Well aware of the influence of the water of the Ganges, to this day they keep Ganges water on the tufts of hair on their heads. They have concluded that this is the ultimate wealth, the perfection of all austerities, and the best means of prosecuting transcendental life.»

Тилака — тело как храм Вишну и защита

Тилака (глиняные знаки) делает тело храмом Вишну: двенадцать мест — двенадцать имён Господа, и это защита «со всех сторон». Гопи-чандана, туласи и святое имя на теле:

«Prabhupāda: It is just like your cross, amongst Christian. It is temple of Viṣṇu. Not only here, we have got twelve place: here, here, here, here, here. Twelve place. The idea is being protected by Viṣṇu from all sides.»

740605 - Conversation A - Geneva·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«In the Padma Purāṇa there is a statement describing how a Vaiṣṇava should decorate his body with tilaka and beads: "Persons who put tulasī beads on the neck, who mark twelve places of their bodies as Viṣṇu temples with Viṣṇu's symbolic representations (the four items held in the four hands of Lord Viṣṇu—conch, mace, disc and lotus), and who have viṣṇu-tilaka on their foreheads, are to be understood as the devotees of Lord Viṣṇu in this world. Their presence makes the world purified, and anywhere they remain, they make that place as good as Vaikuṇṭha."»

Nectar of Devotion, NOD 9 (цитата Padma Purāṇa)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«A similar statement is in the Skanda Purāṇa, which says, "Persons who are decorated with tilaka or gopī-candana (a kind of clay resembling fuller's earth which is produced in certain quarters of Vṛndāvana), and who mark their bodies all over with the holy names of the Lord, and on whose necks and breasts there are tulasī beads, are never approached by the Yamadūtas."»

Nectar of Devotion, NOD 9 (цитата Skanda Purāṇa)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«The purport of this verse is that for any person who simply follows these rules and regulations of decorating the body with the marks of tilaka of gopī-candana or sandalwood pulp, and who puts on the garlands which were offered to Kṛṣṇa, there is no question of being conquered by the spell of material energy. At the time of death, there is no question of such a person's being called by the constables of Yamarāja.»

Nectar of Devotion, NOD 9 (комментарий Прабхупады)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«After cleansing oneself, purifying the body by prāṇāyāma, bhūta-śuddhi and other processes, and marking the body with sacred tilaka for protection, one should sit in front of the Deity and worship the Supreme Personality of Godhead.»

Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 11.3.49 (перевод Прабхупады)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«sannyāsa—by placing transcendental marks of tilaka on various places of the body; kṛta-rakṣaḥ—in this way invoking the Lord's protection; arcayet—one should worship; harim—Lord Hari.»

Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam 11.3.49 (пословный перевод)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«Similarly, we have got dvādaśa tilaka. The same names are there. Lalāṭe keśavaṁ dhyāyet. When dvādaśa tilakas are made, these twelve names are remembered. Lalāṭe keśavaṁ nārāyaṇam athodare (Padma Purāṇa, Uttara Khaṇḍa).»

«Marking the forehead, one should meditate upon Keśava. Marking the belly – Nārāyaṇa. The chest – Mādhava. The hollow of the throat – Govinda. On the right side of the belly – Viṣṇu. The right arm – Madhusūdana. The right shoulder – Trivikrama. The left side of the belly – Vāmana. The left arm – Śrīdhara. The left shoulder – Hṛṣīkeśa. The top of the back – Padmanābha. The lower back – Dāmodara. The top of the head –Vāsudeva.»

«gopī-candana-tale—under heaps of gopī-candana (yellowish clay used for tilaka); āchila—came; ḍiṅgāte—in a boat; madhva-ācārya—Madhvācārya; sei kṛṣṇa—that Kṛṣṇa Deity; pāilā—got; kona-mate—somehow or other.»

Бусы из туласи на шее Прабхупада объясняет ярким образом: это «ошейник» слуги Господа — знак, что у нас есть Хозяин, и защита от посланцев Ямараджа. Туласи священна сама по себе:

«and who mark their bodies all over with the holy names of the Lord, and on whose necks and breasts there are tulasī beads, are never approached by the Yamadūtas.»

Similarly, this belt will save us from being shot down by the agents of Yamarāja. They will see, "Oh, here is a devotee. Here is a devotee."»

⚠️ Красная линия — и есть «истинное значение»

Атрибуты обязательны и несут смысл — но они пусты без преданности, и в этом суть ответа. Прабхупада и Бхактивинода прямо предостерегают от ОБОИХ перекосов: и «знаки без бхакти», и «я уже выше знаков».

«Sanātana-dharma does not mean that dressing in a particular way or keeping a tuft. Sanātana-dharma means to be engaged eternally in the service of the Supreme Eternal.»

711114 - Lecture Pandal - Delhi·Прабхупада

«Even if one is adorned with hundreds of tulasī-mālās and is marked with the Holy Name all over his body, if he is devoid of hari-bhakti, then he is not worthy of the title ‘Vaiṣṇava.’»

Bhaktivinoda Ṭhākura, Garbha-stotra, комм. к стиху 8·ачарьи линии

«Don't become immediately paramahaṁsa—no tilaka, no kaṇṭhī and no bead bag. This is not good. Sāṅketya, so that others may understand, ‘Here is a Vaiṣṇava.’»

750915 - Lecture SB 06.02.13 - Vrndavana·Прабхупада

Итог части 1: носить — да, обязательно (это знак, защита, проповедь без слов); но наполнять их живой преданностью — вот что делает вайшнава вайшнавом. Знаки — оболочка; бхакти — содержание.

Часть 2. Как усилить веру (шраддху) в Кришну

Сначала — что такое вера, потом — как она крепнет.

Определение и место веры:

«This confident, firm faith, favorable to the discharge of devotional service, is called śraddhā.»

Caitanya-caritamrta, Madhya 22.62, перевод (A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«In the beginning there must be faith. Then one becomes interested in associating with pure devotees. Thereafter one is initiated by the spiritual master and executes the regulative principles under his orders. Thus one is freed from all unwanted habits and becomes firmly fixed in devotional service. Thereafter, one develops taste and attachment.»

Caitanya-caritamrta, Madhya 23.14-15, перевод стиха ādau śraddhā (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«If one has full faith in Kṛṣṇa and confidence in Him, one becomes eligible to discharge devotional service confidentially. According to one's faith, one is a topmost, intermediate or inferior devotee.»

Caitanya-caritamrta, Madhya 22.64, комментарий (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«One whose faith is soft and pliable is called a neophyte, but by gradually following the process, he will rise to the platform of a first-class devotee.»

Caitanya-caritamrta, Madhya 22.69, перевод (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«The root cause of devotional service to Lord Kṛṣṇa is association with advanced devotees. Even when one's dormant love for Kṛṣṇa awakens, association with devotees is still most essential.»

Caitanya-caritamrta, Madhya 22.83, перевод (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

Как усилить — по первоисточникам:

Три опоры, и главная — общение с преданными (садху-санга):

«Śraddhā, faith in Kṛṣṇa, is the beginning of Kṛṣṇa consciousness. Faith means strong faith.»

The Nectar of Instruction (Нектар наставлений), текст 5, комментарий (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«When one becomes completely faithful in regard to this instruction, one's strong faith becomes the basis for advancing in spiritual life.»

The Nectar of Instruction, текст 5, комментарий (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«In whatever condition one may be, if he is fortunate enough to associate with pure devotees, then very quickly he is elevated to the second-class or first-class platform.»

The Nectar of Devotion (Нектар преданности), глава 3 (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«Faith is created by association with devotees.»

Bhagavad-gita 9.3, комментарий (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«If one is convinced of this philosophy of life, that is faith. Now the development of that faith is the process of Kṛṣṇa consciousness.»

Bhagavad-gita 9.3, комментарий (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«This faith is attained by the discharge of devotional service, and by chanting "Hare Kṛṣṇa, Hare Kṛṣṇa, Kṛṣṇa Kṛṣṇa, Hare Hare/ Hare Rāma, Hare Rāma, Rāma Rāma, Hare Hare," which cleanses one's heart of all material dirt.»

Bhagavad-gita 4.39, комментарий (Prabhupada)·Прабхупада / первоисточник

«by taking shelter of the proper method of worship in the association of sādhus , gradually destroys his own anarthas and advances progressively to niṣṭhā, ruci , āsakti , and even bhāva .»

Śrī Caitanya Śikṣāmṛta, First Shower Second Stream (Bhaktivinoda Thakura, verified-by-owner)·ачарьи линии (Бхактивинода)

«From association with sādhus comes hearing and chanting, and through the practice of devotional service all unwanted habits and impurities are removed.»

Śrī Caitanya Śikṣāmṛta, First Shower Second Stream (Bhaktivinoda Thakura)·ачарьи линии (Бхактивинода)

«śraddhā is of two kinds, namely tender śraddhā ( komala – śraddhā ) and firm śraddhā ( dṛḍha-śraddhā ). The bhakti that arises from firm śraddhā is exceedingly powerful and by its very nature takes the form of bhāva .»

Śrī Caitanya Śikṣāmṛta, First Shower Second Stream (Bhaktivinoda Thakura)·ачарьи линии (Бхактивинода)

Итог части 2: вера — не «дано/не дано», а живая величина. Она начинается как мягкая (комала-шраддха) и крепнет через садху-сангу, слушание и повторение Святого Имени, вырастая до твёрдой (дридха-шраддха) и дальше — до бхавы. Практика питает веру, вера питает практику.
